## Roaming Model

- **Default**: Free swap between the three protagonists at any hub / between encounters (DC2 Max↔Monica model).
- **Exception**: Specific story stages, trials, or era-locked set-pieces enforce a single protagonist (e.g., a Distant-Past memory sequence you must play as the Evoker).
- **Rationale**: Freedom preserves player agency; targeted locks preserve era/character fantasy and narrative beats.

---

## Arsenal & Legacy

- Each hero maintains a **separate arsenal** — a weapon forged by the Machinist is not directly usable by the Cyborg.
- **Essences** (extracted from items/enemies) are a **global resource pool** — infusable into any hero's weapon sockets.
- **Legacy Traits** (from the Cosmic/Legacy Forge) are **account-wide** — unlocked traits apply to all weapons across all heroes.
- **Memory Cores** (from sacrificed maxed weapons) are global and contribute to the shared Legacy Trait pool.

> This preserves the locked Weapon Legacy pillar ("gear tells the story") while keeping each hero's growth distinct.

---

## Companion Pillar — Removed

The locked **Companion Ecosystem** pillar is **removed entirely** (decision made during character/companion brainstorm).

- No combat allies, no monster/ridepod/robot companions, no party of 3 on field.
- One protagonist active at a time; **hot-swappable** between the three (free-roam, with character-lock exceptions).
- DC2-style **monster / ridepod** concepts are folded into each hero's stances & unique mechanics — e.g., the Evoker's Special-stance combo finishers are monster-themed and strengthened by defeating specific monster types (feeding the broad per-weapon Memory system).
- This reduces scope so design depth goes into the 9-stance combat matrix.

> Design now has **5 pillars** (see `INDEX.md`). The Sets of 3 rule is unaffected — it governs system structure, not companion count.
